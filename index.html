Dr. Maryam Mirzahkani

Fields Medalist

Dr. Mirzahkani shown in a profile by the New York Times. Image © Stanford University
Maryam Mirzahkani was born on May 12, 1977 in Tehran, Iran. Showing early promise in mathematics, in 1994 she won gold at the International Mathematical Olympiad in Hong Kong, the first Iranian woman to do so. In 1995 she followed her first gold with a second gold, and a perfect score, at the International Mathematical Olympiad in Toronto.
Maryam received a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from the Sharif University of Technology in 1999. She completed a PhD in Mathematics at Harvard University in 2004. After serving as a research fellow at the Clay Mathematics Institute and a professor at Princeton University, in 2009 she became a professor of mathematics at Stanford University.
Image © Mott Carter/Clay Mathematics Institute
Dr. Mirzahkani was awarded the Fields Medal in 2014 for her contributions the study of Riemann surfaces and moduli spaces. She was the first woman and the first Iranian to receive the Fields Medal, and remains the only woman to receive the most prestigious honor in mathematics since it was first awarded in 1936.
Dr. Mirzakhani died Friday, July 14, 2017, of cancer. She left behind her husband, a Stanford computer scientist, and a daughter.
